A Geographical Overview:

Lake Geneva, the largest lake in Western Europe, is situated in the southwestern part of Switzerland, bordering France to the west. It stretches for approximately 72 kilometers (45 miles) from east to west, with an average width of 14 kilometers (9 miles), encompassing a total surface area of 580 square kilometers (224 square miles). The lake’s maximum depth reaches 310 meters (1,017 feet), making it one of the deepest lakes in Europe.

The lake is fed by numerous rivers, most notably the Rhone, which flows into the lake at its eastern end and exits at its western end, connecting it to the Mediterranean Sea. The lake’s surface elevation is 372 meters (1,220 feet) above sea level.
